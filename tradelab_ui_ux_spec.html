Core UX rules for the build
01
AI Autopsy is a bottom sheet, not a page
After every buy or sell, the Autopsy slides up as a bottom sheet over the current screen. Student reads it, dismisses it, stays on the trade screen. Never navigate away — breaking flow kills the habit loop. The sheet must appear within 2 seconds of trade execution.
02
RSI is always visible on the stock screen
The RSI gauge sits permanently below the price, color-coded: green (below 50), amber (50–70), red (above 70). Students shouldn't have to look it up — it should feel like a traffic light they instinctively check before buying. This one element teaches RSI passively.
03
Vote countdown is a pressure mechanic, not a bug
The 24-hour countdown on group proposals is displayed as a large amber timer. When under 2 hours, it turns red and pulses. This urgency is intentional — it mimics real market time pressure and drives daily active use. Don't soften it.
04
Green and red are sacred — use nothing else for P&L
Every profit number is #1D9E75. Every loss is #E24B4A. No exceptions, no purple gains, no gray losses. Students' brains need to wire color → meaning instantly. Using these colors for anything else (decorative, branding, status) destroys the cognitive shortcut.
05
Onboarding is one screen, no tutorial
Name + college. That's it. Students get ₹10,000 virtual cash and land directly on the dashboard. No 5-step tutorial, no permission dialogs, no "how it works" carousel. Let them explore and make their first mistake — the AI Autopsy teaches them more than any tutorial would.
06
Trade score (0–100) replaces the P&L as the primary metric
Making money through luck is a 100% P&L but a 40/100 trade score (bad entry, got lucky). This reframes success from outcome to process — the key lesson for a beginner. Show the score prominently. Show the P&L secondarily.
07
Bottom nav has exactly 4 items — no more
Home (portfolio), Trade (search + buy/sell), Group (club), Rank (leaderboard). Everything else is reachable from these 4. Adding a 5th item for Settings or Learn fragments attention. Settings go in a top-right avatar menu.
08
Empty states teach, they don't apologise
Empty portfolio: "You have ₹10,000 to invest. Search any NSE stock to make your first trade." Empty group proposals: "No proposals yet. Be the first to suggest a stock for the group." Every empty state is a nudge to act, not an apology for having nothing.
Flow 1 — First trade (most important for demo)
1
Open app → land on dashboard
Show ₹10,000 cash, empty holdings, "Make your first trade" nudge card
2
Tap Trade tab → search "RELIANCE"
Results appear instantly from /stocks/search. Tap the result.
3
Stock screen loads with live price, RSI gauge, mini chart
RSI is 74 → amber gauge → subtle "Caution" badge. Student notices it.
4
Enter quantity → tap Buy
POST /portfolio/buy — instant response. Cash updates live on screen.
5
AI Autopsy bottom sheet slides up automatically
Shows: Trade Score (62/100), mistake flag ("Overbought Entry"), AI explanation in 3 sentences, "Fix" tip. This is the WOW moment.
6
Student dismisses sheet → sees updated portfolio
New holding appears. Cash reduced. They immediately want to make another trade.
Flow 2 — Group trade proposal (demo highlight)
1
Tap Group tab → create a club or join via invite code
POST /groups/create or POST /groups/join with a 6-char code like TL-X7K2
2
Propose a trade — ticker, qty, rationale
POST /groups/propose. Proposal appears immediately in all members' Group tabs with 24h countdown.
3
Other members see the proposal, vote Yes or No
POST /groups/vote. Vote count updates live. When 2/3 vote yes — trade auto-executes.
4
Group holdings update, corpus changes, vote history locked in
"Approved 3/3 · Executed at ₹2,847 · Pranav proposed" — permanent record. No blame shifting.
